# The 78-week athlete-build protocol

A trackable gym program built around your life: 4–5x badminton during the week, hiking / trail running / tennis on weekends. Designed to maximize fat loss, build durable strength, and improve mobility without wrecking your sport performance.

> **Total duration:** 52–90 weeks (target: ~78 weeks across 4 phases)
> **Visible-change checkpoints:** every 100 days (≈14 weeks)
> **Gym frequency:** 2–3 days/week (never compete with badminton)
> **Built around:** badminton 4–5x/week + weekend trail/tennis

---

## How to use the video links

Each exercise links to a YouTube search pre-filtered to that movement's name. Click the link, then prefer videos from these channels — they are reliable for form coaching and won't lead you into bad cues:

- Squat University (mobility + lift technique)
- Athlean-X / Jeff Nippard / Renaissance Periodization (strength technique)
- Calisthenicmovement (bodyweight)
- E3 Rehab / Knees Over Toes Guy (joint-friendly strength)
- Tom Merrick / Yoga With Adriene (mobility)

Watch one video the first time you do a movement. Re-watch every 3–4 weeks — your eye for technique will improve and you'll catch new cues each time.

## How this works

You already train a lot. The mistake most people make in your situation is adding heavy gym work on top of high-volume sport — you get fatigued, you stop progressing in either, and fat loss stalls because recovery is shot. This program is the opposite: gym sessions are short, dense, and engineered to *support* your sport while quietly stripping fat and building the basic strength/mobility your joints need to keep playing for decades.

Three rules govern everything:

1. Gym never compromises a badminton session. If you played hard the day before, you train smart, not heavy.
2. Progression is checked weekly, not felt. Every set is logged. Every week you ask: did I add a rep, add weight, or move better?
3. Fat loss comes from the kitchen and the weekly NEAT (your sport already covers this). The gym builds the body that *holds* the result.

## The four phases

| Phase | Weeks | Days/wk | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — foundation | 1–14 | 2 | movement quality, base strength, habit |
| 2 — build | 15–32 | 3 | strength + work capacity |
| 3 — develop | 33–56 | 3 | strength + power + leaner |
| 4 — refine | 57–78+ | 2–3 | maintain strength, peak conditioning |

Every phase ends with a deload week (50% volume, 70% intensity). Non-negotiable.

## Phase 4 — refine (weeks 57–78+)

**Schedule:** Auto-regulate based on how badminton feels. 2 days when tournament-prep heavy, 3 days when rebuilding.

You're advanced. The goal here is no longer linear progression — it's maintaining what you've built while peaking conditioning for sport. The structure is the same as phase 3 with these changes:

- Compound lifts move to 3×3–5 at 80–85% — strength maintained, not pushed
- Add a "hard / medium / easy" autoregulation based on [RPE](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=RPE+rating+perceived+exertion+lifting): heavy day only when you feel 8+/10
- Power and lateral work stay at full volume — these have the highest sport transfer
- Conditioning rotates: 2 weeks VO2 intervals → 2 weeks tempo runs → 2 weeks long-easy steady state. Repeat.

### Phase 4 graduation milestones (~day 550)

- [ ] Maintained all phase 3 lifts within 5%
- [ ] Trail run: 10 km comfortably, or hike with elevation without next-day soreness
- [ ] Tennis: full match without late-set fatigue
- [ ] Badminton: 5x/week sustainable without tweaks/strains
- [ ] Photos: lean, defined, athletic look — visibly different person from week 1

## Fat-loss layer (runs across all phases)

The gym builds the body. The kitchen reveals it. Three rules:

1. **Protein first.** 1.6–2.0 g/kg bodyweight per day, every day. Non-negotiable on training days.
2. **Eat at slight deficit, not crash.** ~300–500 kcal/day below maintenance. Crash diets kill sport performance and recovery.
3. **NEAT matters more than cardio.** Hit 8–10k steps on non-sport days. Walk after dinner.

You're already burning huge calories with 4–5x badminton + weekend sport. Don't add more cardio for fat loss — it'll just compromise recovery. Tighten food, hold the line, let the schedule do its work.

## Recovery rules (read these twice)

- Sleep 7+ hours. Below 6.5 = lift gets cut to 60% volume that day.
- If badminton was hard yesterday, push the gym session by a day rather than train tired.
- Deload every 6–8 weeks: 50% volume, 70% intensity, full week. This is when adaptation happens.
- Soreness on the day of a match = warm up longer, lighter, and play. Soreness that lingers 3+ days = you're under-recovering.
- One injury sets you back further than three deloads. Conservative > heroic.
