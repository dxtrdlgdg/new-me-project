A 90-week training protocol · Edition I

Athlete Build

A trackable strength and conditioning protocol for athletes who already train hard in their sport four-to-five days a week. Joint-friendly throughout, paced for recovery, gated on bodyweight where impact matters. The gym never competes with the sport — it builds the body underneath.

Before you start
If you're new to lifting, returning after a long break, carrying significant bodyweight, or managing any health condition, get cleared by a doctor first. Consider basic bloodwork as a baseline. For nutrition, work with a registered dietitian — the training plan below assumes nutrition is handled in parallel by someone qualified to look at your full picture.

Recovery rule

If your sport session was hard yesterday, push the gym session by a day rather than train tired. Recovery is the limiting factor — respect it. One injury sets you back further than three deloads.

Progression rule

Hit the top of the rep range with clean form, then add the smallest weight increment available. Slow, unsexy, works for years. Bodyweight progressions get easier as you lean out — be patient.

Nutrition (talk to a pro)

Training builds the body, food reveals it. Protein 1.6–2.0 g/kg bodyweight daily, modest deficit if losing fat, work with a registered dietitian for anything beyond the basics.

The 100-day rhythm

Visible change compounds every ~100 days. Take photos at week 0, 14, 28, 42, 56, 78. Photos lie less than scales, especially across phases of muscle gain and fat loss.
